Since Apple‚Äôs Worldwide Developers Conference is just around the corner, it‚Äôs time for yet another one of these helpful WWDC articles. I‚Äôve been to almost every WWDC since 2010 and learned quite a bit over the years. So let‚Äôs dive right in!

## Preparations / What to Pack

I assume you already know what to pack for a week-long trip. But for WWDC, it‚Äôs useful to bring a second MacBook and an extra iPad ‚Äî reason being, you don‚Äôt want to install Beta 1 of iOS/macOS-next on your primary device. I‚Äôve been there too many times, and with the exception of iOS 12, I‚Äôve always regretted it. At the very least, wait until Beta 3 before you update your main device! An external SSD will also work well for experimenting with the new macOS release (you might also choose to partition your disk if you trust APFS already).

A battery pack is also super useful. I‚Äôll be bringing my [Anker PowerCore][] because it basically never runs out, unless it‚Äôs charging a MacBook, which it can also do. Additionally, be sure to get an extension cable for your power adapter and a compact US plug converter. Don‚Äôt be the person who uses an oversized adapter that blocks multiple ports. And get an Ethernet dongle. These are in high demand; Apple only provides a few on the tables and there are never enough.

Pain relievers are also a good idea, but you‚Äôll be in the US, where you can buy these and other typical medicines at any supermarket or pharmacy if you find you need them after arriving. Both the Marriott and Hilton are close to the convention center and have the essentials available in their gift stores. Hand sanitizer might also be a good idea.

If you work at a company that has some merch, bring swag! Wear it so folks can easily find out where you work, and feel free to pass some out to ensure your company is memorable. If your Twitter avatar is 10 years old and doesn‚Äôt look like you anymore, [it‚Äôs time for an update][update twitter avatar]. Make it easy for your peers to recognize you.

Got a long flight ahead and have trouble sleeping? Get some sleeping pills for the flight, as some extra rest on the plane will help you survive the week. [Melatonin works really well][melatonin] for me, but some folks prefer stronger pills.

Are you booking via Airbnb? Be sure to ping your host a few days before you arrive to finalize your check in, as sometimes they end up no longer existing ‚Äî an experience which isn‚Äôt so much fun.

Get your badge on Sunday. Sunday is also a good time to get a [T-Mobile Tourist Plan SIM card][], as you won‚Äôt want to rely on Wi-Fi alone. Switch to an eSIM if you want iMessage to work. The Dual SIM feature on the latest generation iPhone is really useful here.

## The Keynote

Should you stand in line all night for the keynote?

![](/assets/img/2019/wwdc-tips-2019-edition/apple-no.gif)

Don‚Äôt queue. This hasn‚Äôt been necessary for many years now, yet folks still queue the night before and sacrifice a lot of sleep. The really interesting keynote is the Platforms State of the Union, which takes place in the afternoon, and you‚Äôll pass out before it happens if you don‚Äôt get a good night‚Äôs rest. Plus, the week will be exhausting enough without the unnecessary loss of sleep! However, if you really want to stand in line, come an hour early to get some queuing experience.

Once you‚Äôre in, go to the toilet before the event starts, or you‚Äôll have to choose between either suffering or annoying people ‚Äî the rows are pretty long. Get a jacket if you‚Äôre not used to American air conditioning, or have a Swift project ready to compile for some extra warmth üòâ. And make sure you‚Äôre at the right venue :P

## The Food

The food at WWDC isn‚Äôt great, but it‚Äôs fine, really. It usually consists of some form of a sandwich, a side, and something sweet. Eat a good breakfast every morning and you‚Äôll be OK. And there‚Äôs always [Odwalla][] if you need an extra dose of <s>vitamins</s> sugar. It‚Äôs easy to go overboard on sugar, but I suggest moderation, or else you‚Äôll feel terrible after the week is over.

## The Sessions

It‚Äôs OK to miss sessions. They are all recorded (with the exception of lunchtime sessions). Apple sometimes does cut out parts (such as accidental CocoaPods references), but for the most part, you‚Äôre better off with a video. Videos can be paused or sped up, and you can watch them when it best suits you. I suggest you only pick a few sessions for the week, and be sure they‚Äôre the ones that really interest you. During WWDC 2018, I only attended one regular session ([Hi, Tyler!][hi tyler]), and I instead spent most of the time in the labs and talking to people.

If you enjoyed a session, tweet to the engineer who did it! Apple folks put in an intense amount of work and huge number of hours to make WWDC happen, so showing appreciation will be very welcome.

## Lunchtime Sessions

There are [three lunchtime sessions][]. They are usually fantastic. They are never recorded. You won‚Äôt want to miss them.

## The Labs

You‚Äôve probably already read that the labs are where the real value of WWDC is. If you really want folks to be able to help you, come prepared. [Have a radar number and an example project ready][writing good bug reports]. Have a charged MacBook with you that can run the example. Labs are open for everyone, but for popular labs, you should be on time, or else you‚Äôll have to wait for some time.

At PSPDFKit, we have a shared list where we‚Äôll, over the course of the year, add all the things we want to talk about with engineers, and we add our notes as we‚Äôre trying to get answers in the labs. Sometimes the lab you need will only be there once or twice in the week, so prioritize labs over sessions. For us, UIKit and compiler labs are the most useful, but this really depends on your project. UIKit is definitely one of the busiest labs there is!

## UI Labs and Special Appointments

Throughout the week, there will be a few [special labs that require an appointment][]. These appointments fill up _really_ quickly, so you need to sign up at exactly 0700, or whenever slots open for the day. They can be booked via the WWDC app or website. Be on time and you might secure a spot. Generally, the UI labs are 30-minute sessions, so the actual feedback is very limited ‚Äî it can be useful, but don‚Äôt expect a full app review. Come with a specific question you want an expert opinion on, and you‚Äôll certainly get something out of it.

## Store and Swag

Every year, Apple has a WWDC store with tons of [swag][]. While the collection changes daily, it‚Äôs a good idea to go there early. Tuesday or Wednesday are good days, as any time after that, the typical shirt/jacket sizes will be sold out.

## Network and Make Friends

Walk around and say hi to people you know and start talking with people you don‚Äôt know! ‚ÄúWhat did you think of the keynote?‚Äù ‚ÄúWhat‚Äôs the feature you‚Äôre most excited about?‚Äù ‚ÄúWhat are you working on these days?‚Äù ‚Äî it‚Äôs easy to start a conversation. Take selfies so you remember folks. Tweet a [#picswithDave][].

Aside from WWDC, there‚Äôs also [AltConf, Layers, and quite a few more events][more events] going on that are worth checking out. I‚Äôll speak at the [CI Best Practices for iOS and macOS App Development Panel on Tuesday at 4pm][altconf]. Be sure to check out their schedule, as there might be talks you‚Äôll like.
